Capital: Columbia · South · Admitted 1788

Native peoples

Indigenous peoples have inhabited South Carolina for thousands of years before European arrival.

European colonization

European exploration began in the colonial era.

Statehood

South Carolina was admitted to the Union in 1788 as one of the original 13 states.

Civil War era

South Carolina seceded from the Union and joined the Confederacy. The Civil War (1861-1865) left lasting economic and demographic marks on the state.

Modern era

Today South Carolina has a population of 5 to 10 million and ranks #40 of 50 by area. The state economy, has strong agricultural roots. The capital Columbia and largest city Columbia anchor the modern political and economic life.
